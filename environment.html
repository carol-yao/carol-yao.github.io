Environment Canada Challenge

Working in a team of 5, we designed a browser extension to suggest sustainable alternatives while shopping online. We won 3rd place ($2k prize) in Environment Canada’s 24 hour design challenge.

The Challenge

The goal of this challenge was to create a digital solution and plan to change individual behaviours for pollution prevention and to engage them with Environment Canada’s Pollution Prevention Resource Finder.

The theme of this event was specifically to stop the usage of single use plastics but the solution should be general enough to be expanded into other areas.

The Design Question

“How might we engage youths to stop using single use plastics?”

Constraints and Requirements

  • Tech must be implementable in 1 year
  • A solution that targets consumer behaviour
  • Have measurable data to collect from consumers
  • Pollution prevention solution, not a reactive solution (i.e recycling)
  • Raise awareness about P2Finder resource
  • Submission video, business plan, low-fi prototype

My Role

We worked together to ideate and brainstorm solutions. My main area of focus was considering the technical feasibility and the submission video (that got us to the finals). The visual and interaction design was worked on by myself and one other team member.

Business and Marketing Plan

For our solution, we wanted to target students so our marketing strategy was to partner with universities and leverage social media. Since students are focused on saving money and convenience, the marketing campaigns would highlight the financial gains from making sustainable purchases, without compromising convenience.

Currently, there are no incentives for students to reduce their plastic waste, we wanted to incentivize them by adding a rewards program. We would partner with sustainable companies to promote their products in the suggestions (at a cost) so this revenue can be used to create rewards for students. Additionally, we added a social aspect to the product so students can share their positive environmental impact and choices so they can feel validation from peers and to start conversations about sustainability.
